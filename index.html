An introduction to the main physical disc-based media formats in home entertainment for the past few years

Video CD

  • the original home video format
  • most movies are split into 2 discs
  • cheaper than the others
  • slowly declining but it's still used for karaoke

DVD

  • also known as "DVD-Video"
  • in most cases, DVDs contains multiple audio and subtitles
  • interactive menus
  • still releasing today

Blu-ray Disc

  • same as DVD, but with Full HD video on it
  • still releasing today
  • another version called "Ultra HD Blu-Ray" exists with 4K video on it

And now, here comes the menus.

Most DVDs and Blu-ray Discs carries menus, allowing users to choose what they want to watch from the disc. From the early 2000s until mid-2010s, movie studios made special animations for the release of the movie itself for home video. ...and these are the bootlegs.

Technically, it's always sold by street vendors on those night markets (pasar malam in Malay). But, here are some facts about those bootleg DVDs...

They usually have bad subtitle translations.

Since I have only some of those discs "surviving" at my room (including Mae Nak and Barbie's Thumbelina), but the subtitles are directly "thrown" into a translation service, such as Google Translate. Since translation technology isn't that advance that time, it usually gives out badly-made translations.

Quality

Most of them are recorded in theaters. Since recording in theater is illegal, they have to do it secretly, which caused the bad audio and video quality. Subtitles on the DVD itself are mostly transcribed using a "text-to-speech" service, which makes the subtitles inaccurate for most of the times.

Bootlegs has begun decades ago

Conclusion

Although there is a rise on online streaming services such as Netflix and Disney+, studios still release their movies and shows on physical media due to its better picture and sound quality, bonus features, commentary and so on. There's even a community which collects these type of media formats. In conclusion, physical media is still alive when there's a need for watching stuff without even subscribing to streaming services.
